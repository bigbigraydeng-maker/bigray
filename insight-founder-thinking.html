Founder Thinking Across Markets 跨市场的创始人思维

How to think strategically when building ventures that span multiple countries and cultures. 如何在构建跨越多个国家和文化的创业项目时进行战略思考。

Ray Deng · March 2026 Ray Deng · 2026年3月

In this article 本文目录

  • Market Differences 市场差异
  • Cultural Context 文化语境
  • Localize vs Standardize 本地化与标准化
  • Founder's Mindset 创始人心态

Building a venture in one market is hard enough. Building across China, New Zealand, and Australia simultaneously requires a different kind of thinking altogether. After twenty years of doing exactly this, I've developed a framework for how founders should approach multi-market ventures. 在一个市场创业已经够难了。同时在中国、新西兰和澳大利亚建立业务，则需要一种完全不同的思维方式。在二十年的跨境创业实践后，我总结了一套创始人应如何经营跨市场业务的思维框架。

Understanding market differences between China, NZ, and Australia 理解中国、新西兰和澳大利亚之间的市场差异

Most founders make the mistake of treating different markets as variations of the same thing. They assume that a product that works in China can be transplanted to New Zealand with a few tweaks, or that Australian consumer behavior mirrors what they've seen in Auckland. This is fundamentally wrong. 大多数创始人犯的错误是把不同市场当成同一事物的变体。他们假设一个在中国成功的产品只需稍加调整就能移植到新西兰，或者澳大利亚的消费者行为与奥克兰如出一辙。这从根本上就是错误的。

China operates at scale and speed. The competitive intensity is extraordinary, and the technology ecosystem moves faster than anywhere else. New Zealand is the opposite: a small, relationship-driven market where trust is earned slowly and reputation matters more than marketing budgets. Australia sits in between, with enough scale to require systems but enough cultural nuance to punish generic approaches. 中国以规模和速度运作。竞争强度非凡，技术生态系统的发展速度超过其他任何地方。新西兰则恰恰相反：这是一个小型、关系驱动的市场，信任需要慢慢建立，声誉比营销预算更重要。澳大利亚处于两者之间，既有足够的规模需要系统化运作，又有足够的文化细微差别来惩罚那些笼统的策略。

  • China: speed-first, platform-centric, massive scale, WeChat ecosystem dominance 中国：速度优先，平台中心化，大规模，微信生态主导
  • New Zealand: trust-first, relationship-driven, niche markets, word-of-mouth critical 新西兰：信任优先，关系驱动，利基市场，口碑至关重要
  • Australia: compliance-heavy, brand-conscious, competitive but approachable 澳大利亚：合规要求高，品牌意识强，竞争激烈但可进入

The founder who understands these differences doesn't try to force-fit one model across all three. Instead, they build a core capability that can be expressed differently in each context. 理解这些差异的创始人不会试图将一个模式强行套用到三个市场。相反，他们会构建一种核心能力，然后在每个环境中以不同方式表达。

Building for cultural context 基于文化语境构建

Cultural context isn't just about language translation. It's about understanding how people make decisions, what they value, and how trust is established. In my experience building migration platforms and property technology products, the biggest failures always come from cultural blindness. 文化语境不仅仅是语言翻译的问题。它关系到理解人们如何做决定、他们重视什么、以及信任是如何建立的。在我构建移民平台和房产科技产品的经验中，最大的失败总是源于文化盲区。

Chinese users expect comprehensive information upfront. They want to see every detail, every credential, every success story before they engage. New Zealand users are the opposite: they're put off by information overload and prefer a clean, straightforward experience. Australian users want credibility signals but are skeptical of anything that feels too polished or too good to be true. 中国用户期望预先获得全面的信息。他们希望在参与之前看到每一个细节、每一项资质、每一个成功案例。新西兰用户则相反：他们不喜欢信息过载，偏好简洁直接的体验。澳大利亚用户需要可信度信号，但对任何看起来过于精致或过于美好的东西持怀疑态度。

Building for cultural context means designing separate user journeys, not just translating copy. It means understanding that a WeChat mini-program serves a completely different purpose than a responsive website, even if they deliver the same underlying service. 基于文化语境构建意味着设计不同的用户旅程，而不仅仅是翻译文案。这意味着理解微信小程序和响应式网站服务于完全不同的目的，即使它们提供的底层服务相同。

The best cross-border products don't feel "translated." They feel native to each market. This requires deep empathy and genuine cultural fluency, not just bilingual staff. 最好的跨境产品不会让人觉得是"翻译过来的"。它们在每个市场中都给人以本地化的感觉。这需要深层的共情能力和真正的文化流利度，而不仅仅是双语员工。

The founder who succeeds across markets is not the one who builds one product for everyone, but the one who builds one capability expressed in many ways. 在多个市场成功的创始人，不是那个为所有人打造一个产品的人，而是那个将一种能力以多种方式表达的人。

When to localize vs when to standardize 何时本地化，何时标准化

This is the question I get asked most often, and the answer is deceptively simple: standardize the infrastructure, localize the experience. Your data models, your APIs, your compliance engines, your core business logic should be unified. But everything the user touches should feel local. 这是我被问到最多的问题，答案看似简单：标准化基础设施，本地化体验。你的数据模型、API、合规引擎、核心业务逻辑应该统一。但用户接触到的所有东西都应该感觉是本地的。

  • Standardize: backend systems, data architecture, security protocols, reporting frameworks 标准化：后端系统、数据架构、安全协议、报告框架
  • Localize: user interface, communication channels, payment methods, customer support 本地化：用户界面、沟通渠道、支付方式、客户支持
  • Adapt: pricing strategy, go-to-market approach, partnership models, marketing messaging 适应性调整：定价策略、市场推广方式、合作模式、营销信息

The trap many founders fall into is localizing too early or standardizing too aggressively. If you localize before you have product-market fit in at least one market, you're spreading resources thin. If you standardize everything, you lose the cultural resonance that makes users choose you over local competitors. 许多创始人掉入的陷阱是过早本地化或过度标准化。如果你在至少一个市场获得产品市场契合之前就开始本地化，你就是在分散资源。如果你标准化一切，你就会失去让用户选择你而非本地竞争对手的文化共鸣。

My approach is to win one market first, deeply understand the core value proposition, then carefully expand. When I built Compass Immigration, we started in New Zealand, understood exactly what Chinese migrants needed, and only then designed the system architecture to support expansion across the corridor. 我的方法是先赢得一个市场，深入理解核心价值主张，然后谨慎扩展。当我创建指南针移民时，我们从新西兰起步，准确理解了中国移民的需求，然后才设计系统架构以支持整个走廊的扩展。

The founder's mindset for cross-border execution 跨境执行的创始人心态

Cross-border venture building demands a particular kind of mental toughness. You're dealing with multiple regulatory environments, different time zones, cultural misunderstandings, and the constant temptation to simplify by retreating to a single market. The founders who succeed share several characteristics. 跨境创业需要一种特殊的心理韧性。你要应对多种监管环境、不同时区、文化误解，以及不断出现的退缩到单一市场以简化事务的诱惑。成功的创始人有几个共同特征。

  • Comfort with ambiguity: cross-border ventures never have clean answers, and you must act on incomplete information 对模糊性的适应：跨境创业从来没有简单明了的答案，你必须在信息不完整的情况下行动
  • Cultural humility: assuming you understand a market because you speak the language is a dangerous mistake 文化谦逊：因为会说某种语言就假设你了解一个市场，这是一个危险的错误
  • Systems thinking: you need to see how changes in one market ripple across others 系统思维：你需要看到一个市场的变化如何波及其他市场
  • Patience with speed: move fast on execution, but be patient with relationship building 在速度中保持耐心：在执行上快速行动，但在建立关系方面保持耐心

The most important lesson I've learned is that cross-border success is not a sprint or a marathon. It's a series of sprints with long periods of patient relationship building in between. The founder who can switch between these two modes will outperform those who only know one speed. 我学到的最重要的一课是，跨境成功既不是短跑也不是马拉松。它是一系列的冲刺，中间穿插着长期的耐心关系构建。能够在这两种模式之间切换的创始人，将超越那些只知道一种速度的人。

Twenty years in, I'm still learning. Every new venture teaches me something about how markets differ, how cultures connect, and how technology can bridge the gaps that geography creates. The founder's greatest asset isn't knowledge—it's the willingness to keep learning. 二十年过去了，我仍在学习。每一个新的创业项目都教会我关于市场差异、文化连接，以及技术如何弥合地理造成的鸿沟的新知识。创始人最大的资产不是知识——而是持续学习的意愿。

Cross-Border Opportunity Design 跨境机会设计

A framework for identifying and capturing opportunities at the intersection of markets. 一个在市场交叉点识别和把握机会的框架。

Ray Deng · March 2026 Ray Deng · 2026年3月

In this article 本文目录

  • Opportunity Gap 机会缺口
  • Information Asymmetry 信息不对称
  • Evaluation Framework 评估框架

The best cross-border opportunities don't announce themselves. They hide in the gaps between markets, in the friction that people accept as normal, and in the information asymmetries that persist because nobody has bridged them yet. After twenty years of finding and building on these opportunities, I've developed a systematic way to identify them. 最好的跨境机会不会主动出现。它们隐藏在市场之间的缝隙中，隐藏在人们习以为常的摩擦中，隐藏在因为没人去弥合而持续存在的信息不对称中。在二十年的发现和构建这些机会的经验之后，我总结了一种系统性的识别方法。

The opportunity gap between markets 市场之间的机会缺口

Every pair of markets has gaps. Things that exist in one market but not the other. Services that are mature in China but nonexistent in New Zealand. Technology that works well in Australia but hasn't been adapted for Chinese users. These gaps are where cross-border opportunities live. 每对市场之间都有缺口。一个市场存在但另一个不存在的事物。在中国成熟但在新西兰不存在的服务。在澳大利亚运作良好但尚未针对中国用户进行适配的技术。这些缺口就是跨境机会所在。

But not every gap is an opportunity. Some gaps exist because there's no demand on the other side. Some exist because regulatory barriers make them impossible to bridge. Some exist because the economics don't work at the scale of the smaller market. The skill of cross-border opportunity design is distinguishing between gaps that are opportunities and gaps that are traps. 但并非每个缺口都是机会。有些缺口存在是因为另一边没有需求。有些是因为监管壁垒使其无法弥合。有些是因为在较小市场的规模下经济模型不成立。跨境机会设计的核心技能，是区分哪些缺口是机会，哪些缺口是陷阱。

  • Demand gaps: services people actively seek but cannot access across borders 需求缺口：人们主动寻求但无法跨境获取的服务
  • Efficiency gaps: processes that take weeks in one market but days in another 效率缺口：在一个市场需要数周但在另一个市场只需几天的流程
  • Trust gaps: where lack of reliable information prevents cross-border transactions 信任缺口：缺乏可靠信息阻碍了跨境交易
  • Technology gaps: tools common in one market that haven't crossed borders yet 技术缺口：在一个市场常见但尚未跨境的工具

The most profitable opportunities sit at the intersection of multiple gap types. When there's a demand gap, a trust gap, and a technology gap all pointing at the same problem, you've found something worth building. 最有利可图的机会位于多种缺口类型的交叉点。当需求缺口、信任缺口和技术缺口都指向同一个问题时，你就找到了值得构建的东西。

Information asymmetry as advantage 信息不对称作为优势

The single greatest advantage in cross-border business is information asymmetry. Knowing things that people on the other side of the border don't know, and building systems that bridge that knowledge gap. This isn't about hoarding secrets. It's about understanding that information flows between markets are surprisingly poor, even in the age of the internet. 跨境商业中最大的优势是信息不对称。了解对方市场的人不知道的事情，并构建弥合知识差距的系统。这不是关于囤积秘密，而是理解在互联网时代，市场之间的信息流动依然出奇地差。

Chinese families considering migration to New Zealand often rely on outdated information from WeChat groups and secondhand advice. New Zealand property developers don't understand how Chinese investors evaluate opportunities. Australian employers struggle to assess qualifications from Chinese universities. These are all information asymmetry problems, and they persist because language and cultural barriers make information transfer inefficient. 考虑移民新西兰的中国家庭往往依赖微信群中过时的信息和二手建议。新西兰房地产开发商不了解中国投资者如何评估机会。澳大利亚雇主很难评估中国大学的资质。这些都是信息不对称问题，它们之所以持续存在，是因为语言和文化壁垒使信息传递效率低下。

The entrepreneur who can systematize the reduction of information asymmetry has a durable competitive advantage. This is exactly what I've done with platforms like Compass Immigration and our property technology ventures. We don't just translate information. We contextualize it, verify it, and deliver it in formats that each market trusts. 能够系统性地减少信息不对称的企业家拥有持久的竞争优势。这正是我通过指南针移民和房产科技项目所做的。我们不仅仅是翻译信息，而是将其语境化、验证并以每个市场信任的格式呈现。

Information asymmetry is not a bug in global markets. It's a feature that creates opportunity for those who can bridge it systematically. The key word is systematically. Ad-hoc information sharing doesn't scale. Building platforms that continuously reduce asymmetry does. 信息不对称不是全球市场的缺陷，而是一个为能系统性弥合它的人创造机会的特征。关键词是系统性。临时性的信息分享无法扩展，而构建持续减少不对称的平台可以。

The best cross-border opportunities are not where markets overlap, but where they fail to connect. Build the bridge, and you own the traffic. 最好的跨境机会不在市场重叠之处，而在市场未能连接之处。建造桥梁，你就拥有了通行的流量。

A practical framework for opportunity evaluation 机会评估的实用框架

Not every cross-border gap is worth pursuing. I use a five-factor framework to evaluate opportunities before committing resources. This framework has saved me from many attractive-looking dead ends. 并非每个跨境缺口都值得追求。我使用一个五因素框架来评估机会，然后再投入资源。这个框架帮我避免了许多看起来很有吸引力的死胡同。

  • Pain intensity: How badly do people need this solved? Will they pay meaningful money for it? 痛点强度：人们有多迫切需要解决这个问题？他们会为此支付有意义的费用吗？
  • Bridgeability: Can this gap actually be bridged with technology and operations? Or are there structural barriers? 可桥接性：这个缺口能否通过技术和运营实际弥合？还是存在结构性障碍？
  • Defensibility: Once you bridge the gap, can competitors easily replicate it? What creates lasting advantage? 可防御性：一旦你弥合了缺口，竞争对手能否轻松复制？什么能创造持久优势？
  • Scalability: Does the opportunity grow as you serve more customers, or does each customer require bespoke effort? 可扩展性：随着服务更多客户，机会是否会增长？还是每个客户都需要定制化的努力？
  • Regulatory clarity: Is the regulatory environment clear enough to operate confidently, or is there significant policy risk? 监管清晰度：监管环境是否足够清晰以便自信运营？还是存在重大政策风险？

An opportunity needs to score well on at least four of these five factors. If pain intensity is high but bridgeability is low, you'll burn resources trying to solve an unsolvable problem. If everything is strong except defensibility, you'll build a business that gets copied immediately. The framework forces honest assessment. 一个机会至少需要在这五个因素中的四个上得高分。如果痛点强度高但可桥接性低，你会在试图解决一个无法解决的问题上浪费资源。如果一切都很好除了可防御性，你会建立一个立即被复制的业务。这个框架迫使你进行诚实的评估。

I apply this framework early and revisit it regularly. Markets change, regulations shift, and what was once a strong opportunity can weaken over time. The discipline of re-evaluating keeps you from falling in love with opportunities that have passed their prime. 我早期应用这个框架并定期重新审视。市场变化，法规变更，曾经强劲的机会可能随时间减弱。重新评估的纪律让你不会迷恋已过巅峰期的机会。

Case studies from the China-NZ-AU corridor 中国-新西兰-澳大利亚走廊的案例研究

The China-New Zealand-Australia corridor is one of the most underserved cross-border corridors in the world relative to its economic significance. Billions of dollars flow through this corridor annually in migration, education, property investment, and trade. Yet the infrastructure serving these flows is surprisingly primitive. 相对于其经济重要性而言，中国-新西兰-澳大利亚走廊是世界上服务最不充分的跨境走廊之一。每年有数十亿美元通过这条走廊在移民、教育、房产投资和贸易中流动。然而，服务这些流动的基础设施却出奇地原始。

In migration services, we identified that Chinese families making the most important decision of their lives were relying on fragmented, unreliable information. The trust gap was enormous. By building a platform that provides verified, bilingual, continuously updated migration information with professional case management, we created something that neither market had on its own. 在移民服务中，我们发现做出人生最重要决定的中国家庭依赖的是碎片化、不可靠的信息。信任缺口巨大。通过构建一个提供经过验证的、双语的、持续更新的移民信息和专业案件管理的平台，我们创造了两个市场各自都没有的东西。

In property technology, the opportunity was different. Chinese investors had capital and interest, but lacked trusted channels to evaluate New Zealand and Australian properties. Local developers had properties but no effective way to reach qualified Chinese buyers. The information asymmetry was bilateral, and the solution required building trust on both sides simultaneously. 在房产科技领域，机会是不同的。中国投资者有资本和兴趣，但缺乏可信渠道来评估新西兰和澳大利亚的房产。本地开发商有房产但没有有效的方式接触到合格的中国买家。信息不对称是双边的，解决方案需要同时在双方建立信任。

  • Migration: high pain, high bridgeability, strong defensibility through trust and data 移民：高痛点，高可桥接性，通过信任和数据获得强防御性
  • Property: high pain, moderate bridgeability, defensibility through bilateral relationships 房产：高痛点，中等可桥接性，通过双边关系获得防御性
  • Education: high volume, low margins, defensibility only through brand and scale 教育：高流量，低利润，仅通过品牌和规模获得防御性
  • Trade: large market, high complexity, requires deep industry-specific knowledge 贸易：大市场，高复杂性，需要深厚的行业特定知识

Each of these corridor opportunities teaches the same lesson: the most valuable ventures are not the ones with the biggest addressable markets, but the ones where you can build genuine, defensible bridges that competitors cannot easily replicate. Technology enables the bridge, but trust and cultural fluency are what make it durable. 这些走廊机会中的每一个都教导着同样的道理：最有价值的创业项目不是那些有最大可寻址市场的，而是那些你能建造竞争对手无法轻易复制的真正的、可防御的桥梁的。技术使桥梁成为可能，但信任和文化流利度才是使其持久的关键。

The China-NZ-AU corridor will continue to grow in economic significance over the next decade. The entrepreneurs who build the infrastructure for this corridor today will define how these markets interact for a generation. The opportunity is not just commercial. It's about shaping how communities connect across the Pacific. 中国-新西兰-澳大利亚走廊在未来十年将继续增长其经济重要性。今天为这条走廊构建基础设施的企业家将定义这些市场在一代人中如何互动。这个机会不仅仅是商业上的，它关乎塑造太平洋两岸社区如何连接。
